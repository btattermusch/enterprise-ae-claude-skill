# Executive Presentation & Business Case Reference

## Executive Mindset

Executives are evaluating whether this initiative deserves their attention, budget, and bandwidth. They ask:
- Does this person understand my business, not just their product?
- Is this problem worth solving now vs. other priorities?
- What's the cost if we don't act?
- Will this work — do they have proof?

**What executives do not want:** Feature walkthroughs, long company history slides, generic ROI claims, or to be sold to — they want to be consulted.

**The Challenger play:** Bring them an insight about their business they didn't have before.

---

## 30-Minute Executive Meeting Structure

**Slide 1: Their business problem (not your company)**
> "Based on what [Champion] shared and what we've seen in the market, we believe [Company] is facing [specific challenge]. Here's why now matters."

**Slide 2: Cost of inaction (quantified)**
Use their numbers where possible.
> "Companies in your position typically see [X% revenue impact / Y hours wasted / Z compliance risk]. At your scale, that's approximately [$X]."

**Slide 3: Your solution — outcome first**
One slide. Not a product tour. Show the before/after:
> "Our customers move from [current painful state] to [desired future state] within [timeframe]."

**Slide 4: Proof — a relevant customer story**
Pick the most relevant customer (industry + use case match). Lead with the outcome.
> "[Customer] — same industry, similar challenge. They achieved [specific metric] in [timeframe]."

**Slide 5: Why now / roadmap to value**
> "Based on [their business calendar], here's what we'd recommend: [Phase 1: quick win in 30 days, Phase 2: full deployment, Phase 3: expansion]."

**Slide 6: Commercial summary**
One line per key term. Not a pricing appendix.

**Slide 7: Ask — specific next step**
Never end without a specific action and date.
> "To move forward, we'd want to [specific next step]. Can we get that on the calendar for [date range]?"

## Business Case Framework

Build the business case WITH the champion, not FOR them — their involvement increases ownership and accuracy.

**Sections:**
1. Problem Statement — current state, root cause, who is affected
2. Proposed Solution — initiative level, not product level
3. Financial Analysis — cost of problem today, investment required, expected return Y1/Y2/Y3, payback period
4. Risk Assessment — risks of action vs. inaction
5. Recommendation — clear ask, decision criteria, timeline, decision date

## ROI Model (Key Value Drivers)

| Category | Metric | Current State | Improvement | Annual Value |
|----------|--------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Revenue | Win rate | X% | +Y% | $Z |
| Revenue | Sales cycle | X days | -Y days | $Z |
| Efficiency | Hours saved/rep/week | X hrs | -Y hrs | $Z |
| Risk | Compliance exposure | $X | Mitigated | $X |
| Retention | Churn rate | X% | -Y% | $Z ARR protected |

**Credibility rules:** Be conservative. Use customer benchmarks, not internal projections. Let the champion validate each assumption. Footnote your sources.

## Handling Executive Objections

**"We have bigger priorities right now."**
> "Is [problem we solve] something that shows up in your strategic priorities for this year? [If yes:] Then the question is really about sequencing."

**"We tried something like this before and it didn't work."**
> "I'm sorry to hear that — what happened? [Listen fully.] The reason customers come to us after experiences like that is usually [specific differentiation]. I'd rather earn your trust by understanding what went wrong."

**"Just send me a proposal."**
> "I'd love to. To make sure the proposal is actually useful — can I ask just two questions before I put pen to paper?"

## QBR Structure (60 minutes)

1. **Their business update (10 min)** — new priorities? Leadership changes?
2. **Success metrics review (15 min)** — how are we tracking against kickoff goals?
3. **Value delivered (10 min)** — quantify what they've gotten; use their data
4. **Looking ahead (10 min)** — their priorities for next quarter; where can you help more?
5. **Expansion conversation (10 min)** — natural next investment based on what they shared
6. **Action items (5 min)** — clear next steps with owners and dates

**QBR golden rule:** The customer should be talking more than you.

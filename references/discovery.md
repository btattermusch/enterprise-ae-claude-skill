## 1. Discovery Philosophy

Discovery is the most important meeting in the sales cycle. Its purpose:
- **Build urgency** by making the customer articulate the full cost of their problem
- **Earn trust** by demonstrating you understand their business, not just your product
- **Map the power structure** so you know who to build relationships with
- **Set the criteria** for how they'll evaluate solutions — ideally in your favor
- **Create a champion** by making the contact look smart in front of their organization

**The biggest mistake:** Turning discovery into a feature tour. Every time you say "we can do that," you lose an opportunity to deepen pain.

## 2. Call Structure (60 minutes)

**Before the call:** Research 10-K / earnings calls / press releases; map org chart on LinkedIn; identify trigger events; build hypothesis of value.

**0–5 min: Open and set the agenda**
> "I've done some homework on [Company] — I want to make sure this is a valuable use of your time. Here's what I'd suggest: I'll share one thing I noticed about your business, and then I'd love to spend most of our time understanding your situation. At the end, I can show you something specific if it's relevant. Does that work?"

**5–20 min: Situation questions** — establish current state briefly

**20–40 min: Problem + Implication questions** — the heart of discovery; let them talk

**40–55 min: Need-Payoff + vision** — help them articulate success; connect pain to outcomes

**55–60 min: Next steps** — always leave with a specific, scheduled next step

## 3. SPIN Question Banks

### Situation Questions (keep brief — you should know most from research)
- "How are you currently handling [process]?"
- "Walk me through how your team [does X] today."
- "How many people are involved in [process]?"

### Problem Questions (surface pain)
- "What's the biggest challenge with your current approach?"
- "Where do things break down in that process?"
- "What keeps you up at night about [area]?"
- "If you could fix one thing about [process], what would it be?"

### Implication Questions (deepen urgency — most powerful)
- "When [problem] happens, what's the downstream impact?"
- "How does that affect [revenue / customer retention / compliance / morale]?"
- "What does that cost you in hours per week? What's the dollar value of that?"
- "If this continues for another year, what does that mean for the business?"
- "How does your [CEO/CFO/Board] react when they see this metric?"

### Need-Payoff Questions (build the vision)
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix this, what would it look like in 12 months?"
- "What would it mean for your team if this problem was solved?"
- "Is solving this a strategic priority for [EB/Company] this year?"

## 4. Common Objections in Discovery

**"We're happy with our current solution."**
> "That's fair — most of our customers said the same thing before we worked together. What would have to be true for you to consider making a change?"

**"We don't have budget for this."**
> "That makes sense — nothing has been budgeted for something you're not sure you need yet. If the numbers worked out, is this the kind of initiative your company would fund mid-year? Who would make that call?"

**"We're just doing research right now."**
> "Totally understood. What's driving the curiosity — is there a specific business problem behind this, or is it more exploratory?"

## 5. Post-Call Follow-Up Email Template

Subject: [Company] — Follow-up from today's conversation

[First name],

Thanks for the time today — really helpful to understand [one specific thing they shared about their business].

A few things I took away:
- **The core challenge:** [their pain in their words]
- **The business impact:** [quantified impact they described]
- **What success looks like:** [their stated outcome]

Based on that, I think the most relevant thing to show you is [specific use case], particularly how [Customer X] achieved [specific result] in a similar situation.

**Proposed next step:** [specific meeting type] — does [date/time] work?

One question before we meet: [one strategic question to keep the conversation going]

[Name]

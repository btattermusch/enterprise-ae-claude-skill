## 1. Framework Overview

MEDDPICC is a deal qualification and execution framework for complex enterprise sales. Each letter represents a critical dimension of a winnable deal. A deal with one or more elements at Red is either not real or needs urgent remediation before forecasting.

| Letter | Element | Core Question |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| M | Metrics | What is the quantified business impact? |
| E | Economic Buyer | Who controls the budget and has final authority? |
| D | Decision Criteria | How will they evaluate and choose a vendor? |
| D | Decision Process | What are the exact steps and who approves each? |
| P | Paper Process | What does procurement / legal / IT require to contract? |
| I | Identify Pain | What business problem are they trying to solve, and how painful is it? |
| C | Champion | Who is selling for us internally when we're not in the room? |
| C | Competition | Who else are they evaluating, and why might they win? |

## 2. Each Element

### M — Metrics
**Probing Questions:**
- "How are you currently measuring the cost of this problem?"
- "If we solved this completely, what would success look like in 12 months — in numbers?"
- "What KPIs does your Economic Buyer track that this affects?"
- "What's the cost of doing nothing for another year?"

**Green:** Specific, agreed-upon numbers with the EB's fingerprints on them
**Yellow:** Some numbers exist but not validated by EB
**Red:** No quantification; deal positioned on features or intangibles only

### E — Economic Buyer
**Probing Questions:**
- "Who owns the budget for this initiative?"
- "Has this been funded, or does it need approval?"
- "Who will sign the contract?"
- "Who could kill this deal even if everyone else wants to move forward?"

**Green:** You've met the EB, they've engaged positively, shared their priorities in their own words
**Yellow:** Told who the EB is but haven't met them
**Red:** Don't know who the EB is, or "we can't get you in front of them"

**Common trap:** Your champion says they are the EB but can't actually approve the budget.

### D — Decision Criteria (First D)
**Probing Questions:**
- "What are the must-haves vs. nice-to-haves for this decision?"
- "How will you evaluate the finalists?"
- "Is there a formal scorecard or RFP criteria?"

**Green:** You know all criteria, confirmed you meet must-haves, and influenced criteria to align with your strengths
**Yellow:** Know some criteria but suspect there are others
**Red:** Guessing at criteria; or criteria heavily favor a competitor

**Power move:** Introduce decision criteria the customer hasn't thought of that you uniquely address.

### D — Decision Process (Second D)
**Probing Questions:**
- "Walk me through what happens after you select a vendor — step by step."
- "Who else needs to be involved before you can finalize a decision?"
- "Have you gone through a purchase like this before? How long did it take?"

**Green:** Full map of steps, owners, and dates; mutual action plan agreed upon
**Yellow:** Know high-level steps but not owners or realistic timelines
**Red:** "We'll figure it out when we get there"

### P — Paper Process
**Probing Questions:**
- "What does your procurement / legal / infosec review process look like?"
- "Do you have standard MSA language, or will you use our template?"
- "What's the typical timeline from verbal agreement to signed contract?"
- "Are there any approvals above procurement — board, CFO sign-off threshold?"

**Green:** All paper requirements identified, timeline assessed, no surprises likely
**Yellow:** Started the conversation but haven't mapped all steps
**Red:** Never discussed — this is how deals slip quarters

### I — Identify Pain
**Probing Questions:**
- "What's driving the urgency to solve this now vs. 6 months ago?"
- "What happens to the business if this problem isn't solved this year?"
- "Who in the organization feels this most acutely?"
- "Is this a strategic initiative, or a nice-to-have project?"

**Green:** Pain is explicit, quantified, tied to a strategic initiative, and the EB has expressed it personally
**Yellow:** Pain acknowledged but not quantified; or felt by operations but not EB
**Red:** Pain is vague, no urgency

### C — Champion (First C)
**Tests for a real champion:**
- Will they share internal information (org chart, budget, competing priorities)?
- Will they arrange EB access?
- Will they coach you on how to position against competition?
- Do they defend your solution when you're not in the room?

**Green:** Has power, access to EB, clear personal motivation, and is actively selling for you
**Yellow:** Enthusiastic user but no organizational power
**Red:** No champion; or your "champion" won't return calls during procurement

### C — Competition (Second C)
**Probing Questions:**
- "Are you evaluating other solutions?"
- "What do you like about what you've seen from others?"
- "Have you worked with [Competitor] before?"

**Green:** Know the competition, understand why you win and lose against them, positioned your differentiators in the customer's language
**Yellow:** Suspect competition but haven't confirmed
**Red:** Don't know who's in the deal

**Status quo as competition:** "Do nothing" or "build internally" is always a competitor.

## 3. Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning | Forecast Impact |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| 🟢 Green | Element confirmed, de-risked | Count in forecast |
| 🟡 Yellow | Partially addressed, needs work | Flag; don't count until resolved |
| 🔴 Red | Unknown or problematic | Remove from forecast or escalate |

A deal with 2+ Red elements should not be in Commit.

## 4. Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Champion is not powerful enough | They love you but can't get EB access | Multi-thread; build new relationships above |
| No quantified metrics | Deal stuck in "evaluation" for months | Force a business case workshop with EB |
| Paper process surprise | Deal slips quarter at "final approval" | Map paper process in Stage 2, not Stage 5 |
| No urgency | Prospect goes dark after demo | Re-surface implication of inaction; tie to their fiscal calendar |
| Undiscovered competition | You thought you were sole-sourced | Always assume competition; ask directly |
